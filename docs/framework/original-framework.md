# TRUST framework version 1

## Transparent

The tool's inner workings, data sources, and limitations must be clearly documented and disclosed. Users and evaluators should not have to operate on faith; they should be given the information needed to understand the tool's context and capabilities.

T1. The tool provides clear documentation on its primary data sources and the scope of its indexed content.
T2. The methodology is explained, including the type of AI model used and the general process for generating results.
T3. The tool's known limitations, potential for bias, and update frequency are openly acknowledged.

## Reliable
The tool's outputs must be accurate, verifiable, and consistent enough to be depended upon for scholarly work. The convenience of a tool cannot come at the cost of its intellectual integrity.

R1. The tool generates factually accurate and verifiable outputs, with mechanisms to minimize or eliminate hallucinated citations.
R2. The tool produces consistent and reproducible results for similar queries under the same conditions, supporting dependable research workflows.
R3. When the tool synthesizes information, the resulting summary must be a faithful and accurate representation of the underlying source material, with clear provenance.

# User-centric
The tool must be designed with the needs of its academic users in mind, integrating smoothly into their existing workflows and respecting their expertise.

U1. The tool is fit for its intended purpose and aligns with the research and educational needs of the University of Twente community.
U2. The tool integrates with standard academic workflows through features like support for reference managers and standard data export formats.
U3. The tool is usable and accessible to its target audience, providing a clear interface without requiring prohibitive levels of technical expertise.

## Secure
The tool must be designed to protect user privacy and data, complying with all relevant legal and institutional regulations.

S1. The tool adheres to the principles of data protection by design and by default, in compliance with GDPR.
S2. Users are given clear information about and control over how their data is used, stored, and retained.
S3. The tool's security practices are transparent and comply with institutional policies and national guidelines (e.g., from SURF, EU AI Act, etc).


## Traceable
Every piece of information generated by the tool must be traceable back to its original source. This principle is the cornerstone of academic accountability and is non-negotiable.

T4. The tool provides clear, accurate, and persistent attribution for all sources used to generate an answer.
T5. The path from a user's query to the final output, including the sources selected, is auditable or explainable, allowing users to understand the "provenance" of their answer.
