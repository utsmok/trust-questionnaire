**TRUST framework v2 draft:** *Revision 02-04-2026*

**Improvements in this version:**
*   Clear identifiers: Using TR, RE, UC, SE, and TC prevents the overlapping "T1/T4" issue from the first draft, making e.g. database entry (in MS Lists/Forms workflow) much cleaner.
*   Reduce unrealistic expectations: By replacing the impossible standard of 100% "reproducible results" with "consistency of consensus" (RE2) and adding the engineering concept of "faithfulness" (RE3), evaluators now have a realistic benchmark for modern RAG systems.
*   More explicit ethical considerations: Splitting bias away from general transparency into an ethical mandate (SE4), and mandating cognitive guardrails against automation bias (UC4), brings this framework more in line with the latest academic research on student-AI interaction.

# T R U S T Framework

## **T**    ransparent *(TR)*
The tool's inner workings, data sources, and limitations must be clearly documented and disclosed. Users and evaluators should not have to operate on faith; they should be given the information needed to understand the tool's context and capabilities.

*   **TR1.** The tool provides clear documentation on its primary data sources and the scope of its indexed content.
*   **TR2.** The methodology is explicitly explained, including the specific LLM model(s) used, the underlying RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) architecture, the corpus of data retrieved from, the maximum number of documents fed into the summarizer, and whether Explainable AI mechanisms (e.g., Chain-of-Thought reasoning) are exposed to the user.
*   **TR3.** The tool's known limitations, indexing gaps (e.g., disciplines or languages omitted), and update frequency are openly acknowledged.

## **R**    eliable *(RE)*
The tool's outputs must be accurate, verifiable, and consistent enough to be depended upon for scholarly work. The convenience of a tool cannot come at the cost of its intellectual integrity.

*   **RE1.** The tool generates factually accurate and verifiable outputs, with robust mechanisms to minimize or eliminate hallucinated citations.
*   **RE2.** The tool demonstrates a high degree of *consistency of consensus*; while exact phrasing or source selection may vary slightly across identical queries due to non-deterministic models, the core scientific conclusions drawn do not contradict one another.
*   **RE3.** When the tool synthesizes information, the resulting summary must demonstrate high *faithfulness*—meaning no information is generated that cannot be directly inferred from the retrieved, verifiable source material.

## **U**    ser-centric *(UC)*
The tool must be designed with the needs of its academic users in mind, integrating smoothly into their existing workflows and actively encouraging their critical thinking expertise.

*   **UC1.** The tool is fit for its intended purpose and aligns with the research and educational needs of the University of Twente community.
*   **UC2.** The tool integrates with standard academic workflows through features like support for reference managers and standard data export formats.
*   **UC3.** The tool is usable and accessible to its target audience, providing a clear interface without requiring prohibitive levels of technical expertise.
*   **UC4.** The tool incorporates *cognitive guardrails* to mitigate automation bias. Its interface clearly communicates that it is an AI assistant, warns of potential hallucinations, and actively prompts users to manually verify the underlying sources to support Generative AI Literacy.

## **S**    ecure *(SE)*
The tool must be designed to protect user privacy and data, complying with all relevant legal, institutional, and ethical regulations.

*   **SE1.** The tool adheres to the principles of data protection by design and by default, in compliance with GDPR.
*   **SE2.** Users are given clear information about and control over how their data (including user prompts/queries) is used, stored, and retained.
*   **SE3.** The tool's security practices are transparent and comply with institutional policies and national guidelines (e.g., from SURF, EU AI Act, etc.).
*   **SE4.** The tool actively addresses algorithmic and data bias, ensuring equitable representation of global research, and complies with ethical AI safety guidelines to prevent the perpetuation of prejudices.

## **T**    raceable *(TC)*
Every piece of information generated by the tool must be traceable back to its original source. This principle is the cornerstone of academic accountability and is non-negotiable.

*   **TC1.** The tool provides clear, accurate, and persistent attribution for all sources used to generate an answer, and incorporates mechanisms to display *bibliometric credibility* (e.g., peer-review status, journal impact, retraction alerts) to help users evaluate the quality of the retrieved evidence.
*   **TC2.** The path from a user's query to the final output, including the sources selected, is auditable or explainable, allowing users to fully understand the "provenance" of their answer.
